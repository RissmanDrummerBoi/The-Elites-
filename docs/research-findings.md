## Student Findings

### How issues are reported

Most students report maintenance issues through **physical methods**, including:

- Visiting the maintenance office in person
- Reporting to residence representatives or wardens
- Informal communication (e.g., word of mouth or groups)

This indicates that there is **no centralized or digital reporting system**, forcing students to rely on manual processes.

### Common problems

The most common issues reported by students include:

- Slow response times
- Lack of feedback after reporting
- Unclear reporting procedures
- Negative experiences with maintenance staff

These problems suggest inefficiencies in both communication and process management.

- <img width="1181" height="723" alt="Screenshot 2026-04-27 210815" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/2ef22309-c768-4a71-88c3-297aefe86820" />


### Response time

Survey results show significant delays in resolving issues:

- Same day: 5.6%
- 1–2 days: 5.5%
- 3–7 days: 16.7%
- More than a week: 27.8%
- Over a month: 44.4%

This indicates that **over 70% of issues take longer than a week to resolve**, highlighting a major performance problem in the current system.

## Key Issues/Findings Identified

### Major Problems
- No centralized reporting system
- Incomplete information from users
- Poor communication between students and staff
- Lack of tracking and accountability
- Delays in response time

---

## Refined System Requirements

Based on combined findings:

- Centralized online reporting system
- Required input fields (e.g., room number, issue type)
- Categorization (Plumbing, Electrical, etc.)
- Status tracking (Pending, In Progress, Resolved)
- Notification system for staff and users
- Admin dashboard for management
